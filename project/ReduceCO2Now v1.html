What History Tells Us

Earth's climate record spans millions of years. CO₂ has always been the primary lever. We're pushing it harder and faster than anything in geological history.

CO₂ oscillated naturally in sync with Milankovitch cycles. Warm periods never exceeded 300 ppm. Glacial maxima dropped to 170 ppm, cooling Earth by ~10°C. Human civilization didn't exist yet.

What We Can Do

Solutions That Work

Stabilizing CO₂ is possible — but only with coordinated action at scale. These are proven levers. We can pull all of them simultaneously.

01 / Energy
Reduce Fossil Fuel Use
Transition to renewable energy, increase carbon pricing, and shift incentives away from oil, gas and coal. The energy system is the single largest lever available.
02 / Carbon
Bury Biomass & Capture Carbon
Biochar, enhanced weathering, and direct air capture can actively remove CO₂ already in the atmosphere — not just slow new emissions.
03 / Food
Eat a CO₂-Optimized Diet
Food production accounts for ~25% of global emissions. Shifting toward plant-rich diets, reducing red meat, and choosing local foods makes a measurable difference.
04 / Waste
Reduce Food Waste
Roughly 1/3 of all food produced globally is wasted, representing a massive hidden carbon footprint. Cutting waste at home and in supply chains is high-impact and immediate.
05 / Consumption
Cut Overconsumption
Manufacturing, shipping, and disposing of goods we don't need drives significant emissions. Buying less, buying smarter, and extending product lifetimes all reduce our carbon footprint.
